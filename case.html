Solar Energy: The Bhadla Solar Park, India

Overview: Located in Rajasthan, India, Bhadla Solar Park covers 14,000 acres and has a capacity of nearly 2,245 MW, making it one of the largest solar parks worldwide.

Impact: The park significantly reduces carbon emissions and supplies clean electricity to over 1.2 million households, boosting the local economy.

Technological Advancements: It utilizes bifacial solar panels and robotic cleaning systems to enhance efficiency.

Wind Energy: Hornsea Project One, United Kingdom

Overview: Hornsea Project One in the UK is the world's largest offshore wind farm with a capacity of 1,218 MW.

Impact: It contributes to the UK's decarbonization goals and boosts local economies through job creation.

Technological Advancements: The project features powerful turbines and innovative battery storage systems.

Hydroelectric Power: The Three Gorges Dam, China

Overview: The world's largest power station with a 22,500 MW capacity, serving as a multifunctional facility on the Yangtze River.

Impact: Besides power generation, it improves river navigation and reduces flood risks.

Technological Advancements: It includes advanced turbine and sluice technology.

Geothermal Energy: The Hellisheiði Power Station, Iceland

Overview: As one of the largest geothermal facilities, Hellisheiði supplies both electricity and heating to Reykjavik.

Impact: It supports Iceland's near-total use of renewable energy.

Technological Advancements: Hellisheiði is pioneering in CO2 capture and mineralization into rock.

Biomass Energy: Drax Power Station, United Kingdom

Overview: Originally a coal-fired plant, Drax has transformed into a biomass-fueled power station.

Impact: It has substantially cut carbon emissions and now produces 15% of the UK's renewable output.

Technological Advancements: Drax employs advanced biomass combustion technology.

Tidal Energy: Sihwa Lake Tidal Power Station, South Korea

Overview: The world’s largest tidal power installation, with a capacity of 254 MW.

Impact: It generates power and helps improve water quality in Sihwa Lake.

Technological Advancements: It uses advanced turbines that maximize energy production from tidal movements.
